White Elephant Aspen – Colorado, USA

Aspen gets another shiny new address on 15 June when White Elephant rolls onto West Main Street with 54 art-filled rooms and suites aimed squarely at travellers who like their mountain towns with equal parts powder, whisky and suspiciously good taste. Inside, it’s all timber, stone and enough curated artwork to make you nod thoughtfully while pretending you understand sculpture. There’s also a ski lounge, outdoor pool, LoLa 41° restaurant and a pet concierge for the kind of dogs that definitely own better winter jackets than you.

Romègas Hotel – Valletta, Malta

Malta’s capital has no shortage of old stones and dramatic history, but the new Romègas Hotel gives both a slick modern twist. The 23-room boutique stay revives a 500-year-old palazzo right in the middle of Valletta’s tangled alleyways. Behind the ancient limestone walls, the place leans hard into modern Mediterranean cool, complete with a rooftop pool hovering above church domes, crooked shutters and streets that practically demand a late-night Negroni wander.

Ord Valley Muster – Western Australia

The Ord Valley Muster is back doing what it does best: throwing world-class music, red dirt, campfire stories and Kimberley chaos into one gloriously remote festival. Returning for its 25th edition from 15–24 May, the festival mixed live music with Aboriginal storytelling, rodeos, comedy nights and enough outback scenery to make city life feel deeply unnecessary.

Auko Eco-Wellness Lodges – Phong Nha–Ke Bang

Opening later this year near the monster caves of Phong Nha–Ke Bang, Auko looks like the sort of place where you arrive for “wellness” and accidentally disappear into the jungle for three days. The low-impact retreat will feature 30 tented lodges stretched along the Son River, alongside wellness pathways with names like Still Way, Wild Way and Open Way.

Detour: The Return of the Night Train

Somewhere along the way, travel became obsessed with getting there faster. But night trains are clawing their way back—like the new European Sleeper route connecting Brussels and Amsterdam directly to Prague. It’s slower, slightly chaotic, and occasionally sweaty. But unlike most airports, nobody’s trying to sell you a $14 bottle of water while you remove your shoes under fluorescent lighting.
